DLP User Training & Awareness

Effective user training reduces policy violations, improves data handling practices, and creates a security-conscious culture. Users who understand why DLP exists are more likely to comply.

Training Content by Audience

General Awareness (All Employees)

Duration: 15-30 minutes | Frequency: Annual + onboarding

Topics to Cover:

  • What is sensitive data? (PII, financial, health, confidential)
  • Why data protection matters (legal, reputation, personal)
  • Understanding policy tips and what they mean
  • How to respond when blocked (justification, alternatives)
  • Reporting false positives and getting help
  • Sensitivity labels basics (when to apply, what they mean)

Key Messages:

  • "DLP protects you and the company from data breaches"
  • "When you see a policy tip, pause and think before proceeding"
  • "It's okay to ask for help if you're unsure"

Manager Training

Duration: 45-60 minutes | Frequency: Annual + new manager onboarding

Additional Topics:

  • Reviewing team member DLP incidents
  • Coaching repeat violators
  • When to escalate vs. handle locally
  • Approving legitimate business exceptions
  • Understanding team-specific data risks
  • Leading by example with data handling

Key Messages:

  • "Your team looks to you for guidance on data handling"
  • "Address violations as coaching opportunities, not punishment"
  • "Know your team's sensitive data and where it should go"

High-Risk Role Training

Duration: 60-90 minutes | Frequency: Semi-annual

Audiences: HR, Finance, Legal, IT Admin, Executive Assistants

Additional Topics:

  • Specific regulations affecting their role (PIPEDA, PCI, etc.)
  • High-risk scenarios and proper handling
  • Secure alternatives for sharing sensitive data
  • Working with external parties (lawyers, auditors, vendors)
  • Understanding encryption and access controls
  • Incident response responsibilities

Role-Specific Scenarios:

  • HR: Sharing employee records, termination processes
  • Finance: Payment data, financial reports, tax documents
  • Legal: M&A data, litigation holds, attorney-client privilege

Effective Policy Tips

Policy tips are your primary user education tool at the moment of action:

TypePoor ExampleBetter Example
Warning "DLP policy violation detected." "This document may contain credit card numbers. Verify you're authorized to share this externally."
Block "Action blocked by policy." "External sharing of files with SINs is blocked. Use the secure file transfer portal instead: [link]"
Override "Enter justification to continue." "This action requires business justification. Examples: 'Client requested for audit' or 'Legal review required'"

Configure Helpful Policy Tips

PowerShell - Set User-Friendly Policy Tips 
# Set clear, actionable policy tip text
Set-DlpComplianceRule -Identity "Block External PII" `
    -NotifyPolicyTipCustomText @"
⚠️ This content contains personal information (SIN/Credit Card).

External sharing is blocked to protect customer privacy.

✅ Approved alternatives:
• Use SecureShare portal: https://secureshare.company.com
• Request exception from your manager
• Remove sensitive data before sharing

❓ Need help? Contact [email protected]

Communication Templates

DLP Rollout Announcement

Email Template - DLP Launch 
Subject: New Data Protection Tools Rolling Out [Date]

Hi Team,

Starting [date], you may notice new notifications when working with 
sensitive information like credit card numbers, Social Insurance Numbers, 
or confidential documents.

WHAT'S HAPPENING:
We're enabling Data Loss Prevention (DLP) tools to help protect our 
customers' and employees' personal information.

WHAT YOU'LL SEE:
• Yellow banners in Outlook, Word, and Excel when sensitive data is detected
• Prompts asking you to confirm before sharing certain files externally
• In some cases, blocks on high-risk actions (with alternatives provided)

WHAT TO DO:
• When you see a prompt, read it carefully and follow the guidance
• If you believe something was blocked in error, click "Report False Positive"
• Questions? Contact [email protected] or visit [intranet link]

WHY THIS MATTERS:
This protects our customers, our reputation, and keeps us compliant with 
privacy regulations like PIPEDA.

Thanks for helping keep our data safe!

[Security Team]

Repeat Violator Coaching Email

Email Template - Coaching 
Subject: Quick Chat About Data Protection

Hi [Name],

I noticed you've received several data protection alerts recently. 
This isn't a problem - it usually means our tools are doing their job! 
But I wanted to check in and see if you have any questions.

Common reasons for alerts:
• Working with customer data as part of your normal job
• Accidentally including sensitive info in external emails
• Using personal email/storage for work files

Can we schedule 15 minutes this week to review? I can show you some 
tips to work efficiently while keeping data protected.

If the alerts are interfering with legitimate work, let me know - 
we may need to adjust the policies or set up an exception for your role.

Thanks,
[Manager/Security Team]
