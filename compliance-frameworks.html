Compliance Frameworks & DLP

DLP solutions help organizations comply with data protection regulations by identifying, monitoring, and protecting sensitive data. This page maps common compliance requirements to DLP controls.

HIPAA (Healthcare)

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act requires protection of Protected Health Information (PHI).

HIPAA RequirementDLP Control
Access controls (§164.312(a))Sensitivity labels, encryption
Audit controls (§164.312(b))Activity Explorer, DLP reports
Transmission security (§164.312(e))Email encryption, block unencrypted sharing
Integrity controls (§164.312(c))Block unauthorized modifications

PCI-DSS (Payment Card)

The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard protects cardholder data.

PCI-DSS RequirementDLP Control
Req 3: Protect stored cardholder dataDiscovery scans, encryption, access controls
Req 4: Encrypt transmissionBlock unencrypted email with card data
Req 7: Restrict accessSensitivity labels, DLP policies
Req 10: Track and monitor accessActivity Explorer, SIEM integration

GDPR (European Union)

The General Data Protection Regulation protects personal data of EU residents.

Key GDPR Principles

GDPR requires data minimization, purpose limitation, and individual rights (access, erasure). DLP helps enforce these by controlling data flows and identifying personal data.

PIPEDA (Canada)

Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act governs how private sector organizations collect, use, and disclose personal information.

  • Accountability — Organizations must designate someone responsible for compliance
  • Safeguards — Protect personal information with appropriate security
  • Openness — Be transparent about policies and practices
